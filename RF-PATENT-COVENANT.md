# AOS Foundation — Royalty-Free Patent Covenant

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-06-05  
**Status:** Active

---

## 1. Covenant

The AOS Foundation ("Foundation") hereby covenants that it will not assert any Essential Claims against any person or entity for making, having made, using, selling, offering for sale, importing, or distributing any Conforming Implementation of the AOS Governance Standards.

## 2. Definitions

**"Essential Claims"** means patent claims owned or controlled by the Foundation that are necessarily infringed by implementing the mandatory normative portions of a Released Specification. A claim is necessarily infringed only if there is no technically reasonable non-infringing alternative for implementing the normative requirement. Essential Claims do not include claims that would be infringed only by:

- Optional or informative portions of the specification
- Implementation-specific optimizations not required by the specification
- Reference implementations, examples, or test data
- Non-normative guidance (Part II of AOS-CORE-001)

**"Released Specification"** means a final, versioned AOS standard published by the Foundation under CC-BY-4.0 with a designation of the form AOS-CORE-***, AOS-POL-***, AOS-HARD-***, AOS-PERSIST-***, AOS-CRYPTO-***, or AOS-FORMAL-***.

**"Conforming Implementation"** means a software or hardware system that satisfies the mandatory normative requirements of a Released Specification at any conformance tier (Foundation, Enterprise, or Sovereign).

## 3. Scope

This covenant applies to all Released Specifications published by the Foundation as of the date of this covenant and to all future Released Specifications, unless a specific exclusion is declared during the public review period for a future specification (see Section 5).

## 4. Conditions

This covenant is granted on the condition that the licensee does not assert patent claims against the Foundation, its contributors, or any other Conforming Implementation for practicing the Released Specifications. If the licensee initiates such a patent assertion, this covenant terminates automatically with respect to that licensee.

## 5. Future Specifications

For future Released Specifications, the Foundation will provide a minimum 60-day public review period during which any party may review the normative requirements. The Foundation will disclose any known Essential Claims during this period. The royalty-free covenant extends to the final specification as published.

## 6. Relationship to Code Licenses

This covenant governs patent rights with respect to the normative specifications. Code artifacts (reference implementations, SDKs, validators, test runners) are separately licensed under Apache-2.0, which includes its own contributor patent grant. The CC-BY-4.0 license on the specification documents governs copyright only and does not affect patent or trademark rights — this covenant addresses the patent dimension.

## 7. No Trademark License

This covenant does not grant any rights to use AOS Foundation trademarks, service marks, or certification marks. Use of the "AOS Certified" mark requires participation in the AOS Certification Program (terms published separately).

## 8. Irrevocability

This covenant is irrevocable with respect to any Released Specification, except as provided in Section 4 (defensive termination). The Foundation cannot retroactively withdraw the royalty-free commitment for a specification that has already been published.
